No house · provably settled

Parimutuel: every bet on a match goes into one escrow pool held by the contract. When the result is posted, the winning outcome's backers split the entire pool in proportion to their stake — your payout is your stake × total pool ÷ winning pool. There's no bookmaker taking the other side and no fixed odds: the odds you see are just the live pool ratios, and they move as people bet. The contract can never pay out more than it holds.

The result is the one thing a blockchain can't see, so an authorized oracle posts it on-chain, reading a free public source (shown on each match). The oracle set is configurable and can require M-of-N agreement. You're protected: the oracle can void a postponed/cancelled match, and if no result is posted by the match's deadline anyone can void it — either way every stake is refunded 1:1. If the winning outcome had no backers, the match auto-voids.
