Deepest marches

Why nobody can cheat this — including us

Every leg needs two block hashes. The first was pinned before you could see anything and generates the terrain — that's the road drawn above, and it is already fixed. The second is sixteen blocks later and rolls the dice: hits, drops, variance. You commit your reactions in between. Nobody — not you, not us, not a block producer — can know the second hash while the plan window is open, and the terrain was pinned before anyone could steer toward it. Settling is permissionless: a friend, a bot, or you tomorrow can push the run forward, and none of them can change a single step, because both hashes are already final. Read the design →

What happens while you're away

The march holds for you — nothing is ever rolled without your answers. A leg you have already committed resolves on its dice block whether you watch or not, and the settlement files itself when you return: it plays out exactly as it would have if you'd watched every block. Lateness cannot hurt you — but leave a committed leg unsettled for more than about a day and it passes the chain's block-hash horizon, and the run concludes where it stood.
