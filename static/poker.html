How a hand works · provably fair

Sit down: everyone antes; your browser generates a secret and commits HASH(secret) on-chain — your hole cards are derived from that secret + a future block hash, so only you can see them and nobody can fake them later. Bet: four streets (pre-flop, flop, turn, river). Pre-flop opens only after your hole cards are dealt and visible — no blind betting. Each street has a 2-minute ceiling, but the host can deal the next card the instant everyone's matched (a checked-around street ends at once), so play is fast. Match the street's highest bet to stay in, put in more to raise (raises close in the last few blocks so they're always callable), do nothing to check. You can re-raise any number of times — no-limit up to your STACK (table stakes: you bet what you bought in with). Can't match a raise? Go ALL-IN with the rest of your stack and stay live: SIDE POTS make sure you can only win what you matched from each opponent, exactly like a casino table. Split pots divide evenly. Showdown: one click reveals your secret; the contract re-derives your 7 cards and ranks the full hand on-chain — straight flush to high card, kickers included. Best hand takes the pot; ties keep the first hand shown. The board and each player's hand draw from independent decks, so an exact duplicate card can legally appear — it counts naturally (two K♥ = a pair of kings) and the odds are identical for everyone.
