Provably fair — the chain rolls, not the house

Every roll you make binds to a FUTURE block: die #k = HASH(blockhash(H) ‖ blockhash(H+1) ‖ seat·1000 + nonce·10 + k) mod 6 + 1. Those block hashes don't exist when you tap Roll, so nobody — no player, no host, no producer — can know or steer your dice. The CONTRACT validates the dice you set aside actually score (1s = 100, 5s = 50, three-of-a-kind and up, straight = 1500) and tracks your banked total; a scoreless roll busts the turn. Highest banked score when the play window closes takes the pot; if everyone farkles, the host reclaims. Your browser replays the exact same dice from the block hash, so what you see is what the chain computes.
