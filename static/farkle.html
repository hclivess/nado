Provably fair — the chain rolls, not the house

When you join, you lock in one number: your greed threshold. At the table's settle block your entire turn is auto-played from HASH(blockhash(H) ‖ blockhash(H+1) ‖ seat) — die #k = HASH(seed+k) mod 6 + 1 — and the contract scores it exactly (1s = 100, 5s = 50, three-of-a-kind and up, straight = 1500; a scoreless roll farkles). Those block hashes don't exist when you ante, so nobody — no player, no host, no producer — can know or steer the dice. Once the block is finalized anyone can roll out any seat; the highest banked score takes the whole pot. If everyone farkles, the host reclaims.
