Provably fair — no cheating

The outcome is the parity of HASH(secret₀ ‖ secret₁), and the contract enforces it on-chain:
• You can't bias it. Both players commit a hash of their secret before anyone reveals, so neither knows the other's secret when they lock theirs in — nobody can pick the result.
• No committing after a reveal. The contract refuses any commit once a reveal has landed (the fix for the "second mover chooses" attack).
• Two players only, committers only reveal. A third party can't hijack or spoil a game.
• Sore losers cannot steal. The last revealer sees the result first, so a loser might withhold their reveal — but after the on-chain deadline the player who did reveal claims the whole pot by forfeit. Withholding only loses your stake. If nobody reveals (or no opponent shows), stakes refund. Escrow and payout are enforced on-chain and reconcile exactly — nothing minted or lost.
